This survey included around 70 people. The people in this survey primarily belong to two regions i.e. Andhra Pradesh and Odisha. 85.7 percent of the participants have age more than 31 years while 14.3 percent of the people belong to the age group of 21-30. There are certain observations made in the data collected. Observation formed are shared in the next slides.

Own Survey. Link in the about section.

In the survey, out of 34 people who are diabetic in nature, 22 people have been diagnosed with BP, while 12 were not patients of BP. In the dataset used for Diabetes Prediction (the dataset on which the prediction algorithm is evaluated), we saw that patients with BP don’t have a high correlation with Diabetes. In our survey also, we found that the ratio is not as high as it’s hyped, but there are probable chances of having Diabetes if you are a patient with BP.

Own Survey. Link in the about section.

Of 37 people who are not patient with BP, 65 percent of people at least exercise twice a week. Therefore, exercising at least twice a week is a clear indication of reducing the chances of being suffered from BP.

Own Survey. Link in the about section.

We wanted to establish a relationship between drinking tea and diabetes. We found that of 34 patient diabetics, 32 people drink one or more cups of tea while of 37 people nondiabetic, 30 people drink one or more than one cup of tea. So, drinking tea has no relationship with diabetes. This very well stands in conclusion with the fact that having basic sweet items required in daily life doesn’t result in a direct relationship with diabetes. Cheese. ✌

Of 34 people who have BP, 85.2 percent of people reported that they had work pressure on a scale of 3 to 5 where 1 means low and 5 means high. Therefore, the amount of stress is directly proportional to BP which may have effects on a patient being diagnosed with Diabetes.

About the Project.

The objective of this project is to help patients detect Diabetes. The prediction of Diabetes is done by training the data collected by the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases. All the patients in this dataset are females of age more than 21 and belong to the Pima Indian heritage. In the application, all the predictions are done by training the Pima India Diabetes Dataset. After checking the correlation of all parameters involved in this dataset, the developer has made this application available for males as well. Because the correlation of pregnancy variable with Diabetes is not relatively as high compared to other parameters. The training of this dataset is done using the Logistic Regression model. The results are accurate and there are no heavy biases in the results obtained.
